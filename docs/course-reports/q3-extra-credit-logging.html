Centralized logging with Loki and Promtail

ChefMate course project · Chameleon Cloud (KVM@TACC) · Q3 extra credit

Abstract. This extension adds centralized logging to the ChefMate Kubernetes deployment: Loki as a persistent log backend and Promtail as the shipper. It was outside the lab baseline and improves operability by keeping searchable logs when pods restart or are replaced during debugging.

1. Integration

Deployed in chefmate-logging. Loki runs single-binary with PVC on node1 via local-path; Promtail ships cluster logs into Loki. Artifacts: ansible/argocd/argocd_add_logging.yml, k8s/logging/loki-values.yaml, k8s/logging/promtail-values.yaml.

2. Operability

Prior work relied on kubectl logs, which breaks under CrashLoopBackOff, rapid restarts, or pod rename after rollout. Centralized logs are queryable by namespace and container instead of a single pod instance.

3. Example

MLflow hit CrashLoopBackOff and OOMKilled during Q2 stabilization. After logging was added, restarts could be followed in Loki for namespace chefmate-platform and container mlflow without racing pod names.

4. Validation

On the course cluster: healthy Loki with bound PVC, running Promtail, and successful Loki queries after an MLflow rollout restart.

5. Conclusion

Durable, label-based log search addresses a real debugging constraint on Chameleon and goes beyond the lab baseline with a concrete example from this deployment.
